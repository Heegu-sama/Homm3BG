\begin{multicols*}{2}

The game is structured into Rounds, during which each player will take their own Turn in a clockwise order starting with the starting player.
During their Turns, players will move their \pagelink{Heroes}{Heroes} on the Game Map, construct new Buildings in their \pagelink{Town}{Town}, and recruit \pagelink{Units}{units} in an attempt to fulfill the Scenario's victory condition.\par
Perform the following steps \textbf{at the start of every Round} except the first one:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Flip any previously used Build, Population and/or Spell Book Tokens back to their active side.
  \item Flip any previously used Movement Point (MP) Tokens back to their active, green side.
  \item Regain uses for Expert Effects \svg{expert}.
\end{itemize}
Then, depending on the current Round number, players either gain Resources or resolve an Astrologers Proclaim card:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Odd-numbered Rounds except the first one are \textbf{Resource Rounds}, in which:
    \begin{itemize}
      \item All players gain income from the \pagelink{Mines}{Buildings, Settlements, and Mines} they control.
    \end{itemize}
    \begin{expansion}[before skip balanced=0.4em]{fortress}
      \begin{itemize}
          \item Players draw next Event, see to the right.
      \end{itemize}
    \end{expansion}
  \item Even-numbered Rounds are \textbf{Astrologers' Rounds}.
    Draw an Astrologers Proclaim card\index{Astrologers Proclaim cards} and resolve its effects.
  \item If the Scenario has timed Events\index{Timed Events} marked on the Round Tracker that have now been reached, resolve them.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{\pagetarget{Events}{Events}}
  Event cards\index{Event cards} may be used in multiplayer games only.
  Shuffle the Event Deck during setup.
  At the start of each Resource Round, draw and read the next Event card after receiving Resources.
  The starting player draws first.
  \textbf{Change the player who draws the Event clockwise} every time a new Event is drawn.
  Resolve any effects in clockwise order starting with the drawer.
  Cards revealed as a part of resolving an Event are shuffled back into their respective Decks.

\subsection*{Turn Order}

After the start of the Round, players take Turns in a clockwise order as described in the next section.
After all players have played their Turn, move the Black Cube on the Round Tracker one space forward and perform the start of Round again.
Keep playing new Rounds until any of the Scenario's ending conditions have been met.

\note{6}{
  In the \textbf{Alliance game}, teams take turns alternately.
  At the start of your team's first turn of the Round, choose who goes first and last in that Round.
}

\subsection*{Example Round}

\textit{Alice is playing as the Tower Faction against Bob playing as the Dungeon Faction.
Alice is the starting player.
They are about to start the \nth{5} Round.
}\par

\textit{Before anything else, both players flip their Build, Population, and Spell Book Tokens back to their active side.
All Movement Tokens are flipped back to their active, green~\svg[12]{movement_token_green} sides.
Both players also regain the use of their Expert Effects \svg{expert}.}\par

\textit{Round 5 is an odd-numbered Round, and not the first, so it is a \textbf{Resource Round}.
Both players collect income from their Buildings, Mines, and Settlements.}\par

\textit{Since both Alice and Bob have City Halls built, they resolve their ``At the beginning of each Resource Round'' effects.
The \wikilink{/towns/tower/\#fully-built}{Tower City Hall} allows Alice to either gain 4~\svg{gold} or draw a card from her Deck.
She chooses the latter.
The \wikilink{/towns/dungeon/\#fully-built}{Dungeon City Hall} gives Bob the option to gain extra 5~\svg{gold} or 1~\svg{valuables}.
Bob chooses the former.
}\par


\begin{expansion}{fortress}
\textit{Since this is a Resource Round, an Event card should now be drawn from the Event Deck.
  The drawing responsibility rotates clockwise after each Event: Alice, as the starting player, drew the first Event back in Round 3.}\par

\end{expansion}
\begin{expansion}{fortress}
\textit{It is therefore Bob's turn to draw. He takes the top card from the Event Deck, reads it aloud, and places it face-up so both players can see: it is \textbf{Artifact Merchant} (see the Event card on the previous page).}\par

\vspace*{0.5em}
\textit{Bob draws 5 cards from the top of the Artifact Deck, revealing them to both players.
Among them there is a Major Artifact -- the \wikilink{artifacts/head_of_legion}{Head of Legion.}
Bob goes first, as the player who drew the Event.
  He immediately purchases the Head of Legion, pays 5~\svg{gold} (putting the gold from the City Hall to good use) and adds it to his Deck.
He passes the remaining cards to Alice.
Alice had also wanted the Head of Legion, but it is already gone.
She is not interested in the remaining cards, therefore she passes.
Since the cards have now returned to the player who drew them, the Event ends.
The remaining Artifacts are shuffled back into the Artifact Deck.}\par
\end{expansion}

\vspace*{0.5em}
\textit{Round 5 also has a timed Event marked on the Round Tracker: Scenario specifies that all Heroes gain +1~\svg{movement} this Round.
Bob controls only his Main Hero, so only one extra MP is added, increasing their \svg{movement} from 3 to 4.
Because Alice controls both a Main Hero and a Secondary Hero, \textbf{both} of them benefit -- their movement goes to 4 and 3, respectively.}\par

\textit{As a starting player, Alice now starts her Turn.}\par
